Environment and Accessibility

Generally, the more accessible an environment is, the more active older people may be. 

Convenient to reach to the outdoor environment and public spaces can enable older people to be more active. 

Good health is associated with access to green areas

and the time spent outdoors.

Although older people may have physical difficulties,

they often wish to be more active and mobile.

For this reason it's important to promote safe, comfortable and accessible areas for older people,

which requires surround environment,  

include transport, hospital, social participation,

community services, outdoor spaces and public buildings into consideration.

Urban areas VS Rural areas

Elizabeth Burton,

a professor of sustainable architectural design and health at Warwick University,

pointed out:"we don't have much choice for the elderly at the moment - they either lives in an ordinary house or lives in a nursing home, and not much choice between them."

In addition, most of us believe that

people move from the city center to the suburbs,

from the suburbs to small towns,

from small towns to villages.

But now people have realized that elderly people’s mobility are reduced, they may no longer be able to drive,

their world shrinks - so facilities closer to the high-density population are better."

Professor Jeremy Myerson,

director of the age-friendly design expert at the Helen Hamlin Design Center,

said that “the overall attitude of the British society towards the elderly is unsustainable”.

“It is difficult to care for the elderly – social care and health care,

when they are rarely distributed in rural environments

or gather in seaside towns.”
